## 3. HACKATHON HISTORY — LAST 5 EDITIONS (TECHgium)

The current hackathon is the *first Global EI Hackathon* — separate from TECHgium. But the pattern of winning themes from TECHgium reveals judge mindset.

### TECHgium 7th Edition (2024)
- **Theme:** Mobility, Sustainability, Hi-Tech (UAVs, medical diagnostics, robotics, EV, CV, analytics)
- **Participants:** 36,765 students from 503 institutes
- **Winner:** BV Raju Institute of Technology — *AI-Based Situational Awareness for eVTOL Detect and Avoid Systems*
- **Runner-up:** Sri Ramakrishna Engineering College — *TwinScape Oil Well Monitor*
- **2nd Runner-up:** Amrita University — *Knee Injury Detection System (AI Radiologist)*
- **Prize:** ₹18 lakh total

**Pattern:** Safety-critical AI systems win. AI that augments expert human decision-making wins.

### TECHgium 6th Edition (2023)
- **Theme:** Sustainability, UAVs, medical diagnostics, EV/EV infrastructure, wireless transmission
- **Winner:** Vishwakarma Institute of Technology, Pune — *Alginate-based Bioplastic for food packaging*
- **Runner-up:** Sri Krishna College — *Plug-in kit for EV ready charging*
- **2nd Runner-up:** IIT Guwahati — *Electro-mechanical charging device for TPMS*
- **Prize:** ₹18 lakh total

**Pattern:** Sustainability solutions with hardware proof of concept. AI-enabled sustainability wins.

### TECHgium 5th Edition (2022)
- **Theme:** Digital manufacturing, autonomous systems, healthcare, sustainability
- **Pattern (inferred):** Autonomous systems + healthcare AI dominated

### TECHgium 4th Edition (2021)
- **Theme:** Post-pandemic: smart factories, remote monitoring, healthcare AI
- **Pattern (inferred):** Factory automation + remote diagnostics

### TECHgium 3rd Edition (earlier)
- **Theme:** Industry 4.0, IoT, robotics
- **Pattern:** IoT + edge AI for industrial applications

## 5. PITCH STRATEGY FOR JULY 9

### The 2-Minute Story

> "Every industrial plant loses millions when an asset fails unexpectedly. Today, AI can predict failure — but it can't decide what to do. And it can't prove its recommendation works.
>
> PlantMind changes this. We predict failure with a neural model, score every corrective action with a deterministic formula — the Götze Score — and then *prove* the winning action works by showing you the counterfactual: what happens to the asset if you do nothing, versus what happens when you apply the fix.
>
> The red line is failure. The green line is the rescued asset. That's not a prediction — that's a proof."
